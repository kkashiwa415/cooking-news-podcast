The Musk versus Altman trial is heading into its final week, and the jury's about to decide who actually built what.

We're in week three of one of tech's most contentious legal battles. Elon Musk and Sam Altman have spent the last couple weeks trading accusations about credibility, ambition, and who's really responsible for OpenAI's early direction. The core dispute centers on claims about Musk's involvement in founding the organization and whether he's been truthful about his role and intentions over the years. Both sides have presented their narratives, and now it comes down to the jury deciding which version of events they believe. This matters because it'll likely influence how tech founders think about their legal exposure going forward, and it's already become a proxy battle over who gets to claim ownership of some of the most important AI developments of the last decade.

The outcome could reshape how we understand the early days of modern artificial intelligence, and it'll definitely impact both men's credibility in the industry for years to come.
