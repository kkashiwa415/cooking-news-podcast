In other news, there's a major development brewing in the energy sector that's going to surprise a lot of people. Zap Energy, a fusion power startup that's been hyper-focused on developing fusion reactors, just announced it's pivoting to add nuclear fission to its portfolio. This is a pretty significant move because fusion and fission are completely different technologies with different timelines. Fusion is the long-term play—the moonshot that's supposed to deliver clean, abundant energy eventually. Fission is the proven technology that works right now but comes with all the baggage around nuclear waste and safety concerns. So why would a fusion startup suddenly dive into fission? The short answer is that fusion is taking longer than expected, and there's real money available right now for companies that can deploy fission reactors faster. By hedging their bets and developing both approaches, Zap Energy is basically saying they don't want to bet the company on fusion alone. They want revenue and customers today, not just a theoretical breakthrough ten years from now. For the energy transition, this could actually be good news—it means we might get more nuclear capacity sooner rather than waiting for the fusion moonshot. But for purist fusion advocates, it might feel like a loss of faith. The real question is whether Zap can execute on both fronts simultaneously or whether adding fission ends up diluting their fusion efforts.

For the blockchain space, this is a reality check that separates the serious players from the hype merchants.
